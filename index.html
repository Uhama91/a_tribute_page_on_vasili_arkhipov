Vasili Alexandrovich Arkhipov

Here is a chronology of Arkhipov's life:

  • 1926 - Born in Staraya Kupavna, near Moscow
  • 1947 - Joins the Soviet Navy, rising rapidly through the ranks to become a commander
  • 1962 - During the Cuban missile crisis, Arkhipov, then second-in-command of the B-59 submarine, refuses to approve the launch of a nuclear missile, thus helping to avert a nuclear war
  • 1975 - Becomes commander of the submarine fleet at the Kirov naval base in the Baltic Sea
  • 1981 - Promoted to Rear-Admiral, continuing a distinguished career in the Soviet Navy
  • 1988 - Retired from the Soviet Navy after a long and illustrious career
  • 1998 - Passed away on August 19 at the age of 72, leaving a legacy of courage and dedication in the service of world peace

"Arkhipov's life and actions are a testament to the incredible impact that one man, armed with courage, composure and great foresight, can have on the destiny of mankind." -- Thomas Blanton (former director of the non-governmental organization National Security Archive, at a ceremony in Moscow in 2002)

"Arkhipov was a hero. He saved the world." -- Robert McNamara (former U.S. Secretary of Defense)

"Arkhipov was a man of great bravery and wisdom. His actions helped prevent a nuclear war." -- Mikhail Gorbachev (former President of the Soviet Union)

Arkhipov is a hero of whom we should all be proud. His courage and determination averted a catastrophe of unimaginable proportions, saving millions of lives.
